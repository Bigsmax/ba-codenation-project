1.4 - Describe the ristks that might impact digital projects - what casues digital projects to fail? Why?

Risks that could impact digital projects include, lack of scoping, poorly defined, strategy / vision unclear, internal resistance between teams and organisations, not enough investment in technology, poor vendor selection, and vendor lock-in.

Mainstream companies are more likely to suffer from unclear strategy, poor on-going measurement and lack of alignment in the team. Very often larger tech companies fail to deliver projects due to vendor locking. This is when the cost of switching to a different vendor is so high that the customer is essentially stuck with the original vendor and “locked in" to what may be an inferior product or service. A real-world example of vendor lock-in is in cloud based computing, where software or computing infrastructure is outsourced to cloud vendors, who offers it as a service and delivers it over the Internet. The issue with vendor lock-in is the difficulty in moving to another cloud service provider if something goes awry.

1.7 - Describe leagl guidelines and constraints that impact digital projects - How does GDPR impact digital projects?

The UK Government has put in place tough new measures to ensure the UK is the safest place in the world to be online. Independent regulators have been appointed to enforce new standards and to ensure that digital media companies meet their responsibilities accordingly. The laws in place apply to any digital media & tech company that allows its users to share or discover user generated content or interact with each other online. This means wide ranges of companies of all sizes are in scope, including social media platforms, file hosting sites, advertising agencies, public discussion forums, messaging services, and search engines. GDPR regulations have been modified according to government regulations. As an example, code of practice prevents any media firm from using behavioural advertising. Under existing legislation (the GDPR and PECR) user consent is already needed before behavioural advertising can take place. The GDPR code particularly focuses on areas pertaining to child users, or all users if age is not established.
